Cat's Eye Nebula: NGC 6543

The Cat's Eye Nebula (NGC 6543) is one of the most complex nebulae known, located in the constellation Draco.

It is a planetary nebula, formed from the outer layers of a dying star, and is about 3,300 light-years away from Earth.

The nebula is known for its intricate structure and vibrant colors, which are caused by ionized gases.

The Cat's Eye Nebula is also notable for having multiple rings and knots, making it a popular subject for astronomers.

It was first discovered by the astronomer William Herschel in 1786.
